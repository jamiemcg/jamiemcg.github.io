Jamie McGowan

PhD Candidate - Oomycete Genome Evolution



I am a final year PhD student in the Genome Evolution Laboratory of Maynooth University, under the supervision of Dr. David Fitzpatrick. For my PhD, I am applying a mix of bioinformatics, genomics and proteomics techniques to study genome evolution in Phytophthora and other Oomycete species. These microbial pathogens represent one of the biggest threats to global food security and natural ecosystems. I use a combination of dry and wet lab techniques with the goal of characterising how Oomycete genomes have evolved, allowing them to become such successful pathogens. My research is funded by an Irish Research Council Government of Ireland Postgraduate Scholarship. Prior to this, I graduated from Maynooth University with a first class honours BSc degree in Biology and Computer Science (double honours).
